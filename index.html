Information Retrieval

Using Deep Learning for NLP on CT Free Text

Problem Statement & Dataset

Given free text CT reports of ~122,000 patients, extract the following categorical features

Pleural effusion, Cardiomegaly, Ascites , pulmonary edema , Lymphadenopathy, GB thickening, Hydronephrosis , Hernia , atelectasis, Enlarged Liver, Aortic aneurysm, Atherosclerosis, Previous surgery, Pneumonia

  • Information extraction (IE) is the task of automatically extracting structured information from unstructured and/or semi-structured machine-readable documents.
  • Highly task specific and doesn't extend well to cross domains.

Methodology

  • String matching: Look for words from data dictionary in the report. -- Poor performance. 
  • CNN for Ensemble of binary classifiers using word2vec trained on CT reports--- Average AUC 0.85
  • CNN for Ensemble of binary classifiers using word2vec trained on PubMed and Wiki--- Average AUC 0.96

Natural Language Processing and Deep Learning

  • Used Convolutional Neural Network for sentence classification *.
  • Ensembled 14 binary classifiers + 1 multi-class classifier (pleural effusion intensity) to create 1 multi-label classifier.

* Convolutional Neural Network for sentence classification, Kim, Y, EMNLP 2014

Word Embedding

  • All deep learning models take as input a vector of numbers.
  • For images, traditionally each pixel's intensity is used as input. However in case of text it's not immediately clear how to convert words to numbers that make intuitive sense.
  • Bag of Words, Word2Vec, GloVe etc.
  • We generate Word2Vec embedding for 122,000 reports. [DEMO]

Word2Vec* - SkipGram/CBOW

  • Word Embedding paradigm based on co-occurrence statistics.
  • Given a central word, what's the probability of occurrence of  surrounding (context) words.
  • Typically needs huge corpora to meaningfully train. Entire wiki  dump is generally used to train general purpose word vectors. [3 million words * 300 features * 4bytes/feature = ~3.35GB]

*Distributed Representations of Words and Phrases and their Compositionality , Mikolov, T. et.al., NIPS 2013
